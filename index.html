about me

I’m a PhD candidate at FBK who is expected to obtain his degree in April 2014 from the ICT Doctoral School of the University of Trento. I received my Master in Computer Science degree from both the Georgia Institute of Technology (Atlanta, GA, USA) and the University of Trento (cum laude) in 2010. My research interests include ICT for Development, Living Labs, Social Innovation and Project Management. I am also quite fond of Legal Informatics, HCI and Participatory Design.

I am the project manager of BringTheFood, a mobile and web application to fight food waste developed at FBK in collaboration with Fondazione Banco Alimentare (the Italian Food Bank).

Since 2011 I have bee involved in the Maputo Living Lab initiative in Mozambique, sponsored by the Autonomous Province of Trento and supported by the Ministry of Science and Technology of Mozambique. Since that year I have been actively involved in the organization and teaching activities of the Summer School of ICTs and the Summer School of Ruby on Rails in Maputo, where I taught Agile software development, project management and Ruby on Rails development.

From October 2010 to December 2012 I have been one of the instructors of the Case Studies course of the Professional Master in eGovernment Technologies of the University of Trento.

I am also one of the organizers of the Trento edition of Random Hacks of Kindness (RHoK).

Dwesa

by Aaron Ciaghi - Mar 12th, 2013 -

Last week I’ve been to Dwesa (map) to visit the schools involved in the Siyakhula Living Lab project by Rhodes University and the University of Fort Hare. We had a chance to see the wireless infrastructure put up by the researchers here and we had a glimpse of how life is in a South African rural area.

For about a week we lived in a chalet in the forest without electricity (but very close to the beach!). Quite an interesting experience… During the day we helped out with computer literacy training for community members (some of which had never seen a PC before). It required a lot of patience but our “students” went from not knowing how to turn on a PC to writing documents in LibreOffice. On our side, we ended up learning some isiXhosa and we even got Xhosa names!

Big Change: I’m in South Africa

by Aaron Ciaghi - Feb 19th, 2013 - posted in phd, travel,

I’m in South Africa. I’ll be a visiting researcher at Rhodes University until the end of April. My job here is to meet as many people as possible who are involved in ICT for Development projects (ICT4D). The goal is to investigate which software development methodologies and project management techniques are applied in ICT4D project in order to figure out what works, what doesn’t and especially why.
