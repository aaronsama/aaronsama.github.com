about me

I'm a PhD student at the ICT Doctoral School of the University of Trento, Italy. I'm working in the ICT4G group at Fondazione Bruno Kessler (Trento, Italy) and my current interests include ICT for Development, Living Labs, Legal Informatics, HCI and Participatory Design.

I obtained my BS degree in Computer Science at the University of Trento in 2007 with a thesis on law modeling. I participated to the ATLAS dual Master Degree in Computer Science at the University of Trento and at Georgia Institute of Technology (Atlanta, GA). I received a MS degree in Computer Science from Georgia Tech in May 2010 and I graduated cum laude from the University of Trento in July 2010.

I am currently the project manager of BringTheFood, a mobile and web application to fight food waste.

I am also involved in the organization of Maputo Living Lab's Summer School of ICTs and RHoK hackathons in Trento.

From October 2010 to December 2012 I have also been one of the instructors of the Case Studies course of the Professional Master in eGovernment Technologies of the University of Trento.
